Celebrating Youth Day in South Africa

What is Youth Day?

Youth Day is celebrated annually in South Africa on June 16th. It is a day that commemorates the Soweto Uprising of 1976, where thousands of black students protested against the apartheid government's education policies.

The protest turned violent when police opened fire on the unarmed students, killing and injuring many. This event is considered a turning point in the fight against apartheid and is a significant event in South African history.

"The youth of today are the leaders of tomorrow."

- Nelson Mandela

How is Youth Day celebrated?

Youth Day is a public holiday in South Africa and is celebrated with various events, including concerts, speeches, and marches. It is also a time to reflect on the sacrifices made by the youth of 1976 and to honor their legacy.
